# Where Are Our Girls Going?
 
An independent community research initiative tracking ward level indicators, including school dropout, teenage pregnancy, and FGM/C prevalence, across Narok South Sub-County, Kenya.
 
## About
 
FGM/C is the root cause behind a chain of harm in this context: girls who are cut are typically married off immediately after, ending their education and childhood in the same act. This project exists to make that pattern visible at the ward level, with data, rather than relying on sub-county wide averages that hide which specific wards are failing girls.
 
The findings are taken directly to county government officials and NGO partners to inform targeted interventions.
 
## What this repo contains
 
- Ward level data collection templates and methodology
- Geospatial mapping of indicators (GIS)
- Analysis notebooks and findings
- (In development) An AI agent that monitors ward level indicators for early warning signs and alerts relevant officials or NGO contacts when a risk threshold is crossed, with human review before any alert is sent
## Methodology
 
1. **Data collection** — field visits and GPS location data across wards in Narok South Sub-County
2. **Mapping** — ward by ward geospatial visualization of dropout, pregnancy, and FGM/C indicators
3. **Advocacy** — findings shared directly with county government and NGO partners
4. **Early warning (in progress)** — an AI agent layer for faster, automated alerting
## Status
 
This is an early stage, ongoing initiative. Data collection and government engagement are active; the AI early warning component is in development.

## Note on data sensitivity
 
This repository does not publish any individually identifiable information about girls, families, or specific incidents. All shared data is aggregated at the ward level to protect the privacy and safety of those involved.
